#### Progress
- **What I accomplished**:
  - Started planning how the physical board game concept could translate into a digital experience.
  - Brainstormed a real-time exploration system instead of a strictly turn-based system for the digital version.
  - Designed the concept of a **noise tracking system** where each step a player takes increases a noise meter that may attract the monster.
  - Considered how the monster AI could dynamically respond to sound, players proximity, or random movement patterns.
  - Reflected on feedback suggesting multiple endings for the game and evaluated whether it should be implemented in the physical version.
- **Challenges faced**:
  - One challenge was determining how closely the digital version should follow the physical board game rules. Some mechanics that work well in tabletop games may feel slow or restrictive in a digital format.
  -  Another challenge was deciding how complex the monster behavior should be without making the game frustrating for players.
  -  I also received feedback recommending multiple endings, but my professor advised keeping the physical board game simpler with one clear ending.
- **Solutions**:
  - To adapt the gameplay for a digital environment, I considered making exploration continuous instead of turn-based, allowing players to move freely through rooms while the environment reacts dynamically.
  - The monster behavior could be governed by simple AI rules such as following noise signals or occassionally wandering randomly to maintain suspense.
  - Instead of adding multiple endings to the physical game, I decided to keep one main victory condition and include a reflection explaining how additional endings could be implemented in future versions.

#### Learnings
- This module helped me understand how game mechanics often need to change when transitioning from a physical board game to a digital game.
- I learned that digital games allow for more dynamic systems such as AI movement, sound tracking, and real-time exploration.
- I also realized that simplicity is important in physical board games to keep gameplay understandable and accessible.

- The monster in the digital version could have **loose AI behvior**, meaning it may wander the mansion but become more aggressive when players create noise or interact with objects.

- I also thought about how the digital version could automatically manage things that are difficult in tabletop games, such as tracking player noise levels or triggering hidden events.
