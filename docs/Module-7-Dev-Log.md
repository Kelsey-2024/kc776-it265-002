#### Goals for this Module
  - [X] Playtest the physical board game prototype
  - [X] Gather feedback on mechanics and gameplay flow 
  - [X] Identify improvements for both physical and digital version

#### Progress
  - **What I accomplished**:
    - Conducted playtesting sessions using the completed prototype
    - Tested core mechanics including:
      - Movement using D6 dice
      - Tiles-based exploration
      - Event, item, and trap systems
      - Noise meter functionality
    - Observed player behavior and pacing during gameplay
    - Began transitioning ideas toward a digital version of the game
  - **Challenges faced**:
    - The noise meter caused the game to end too quickly (sometimes within two turns)
    - Players felt pressured too early, limiting exploration and strategy
    - Uncertainty around how players should reveal/open rooms
    - Lack of clarity in how the digital version should differ from the physical version
    - Balancing randomness (dice + events) with player control
  - **Solutions**:
    - Proposed increasing the noise meter cap significantly being that the board is big. (noise meter went from 10 to 25)
    - Considered adjusting noise rules:
      - Noise only increases when rolling a 6 (instead of every turn)
      - This introduces risk-based mechanics instead of constant punishment
    - Reinforced the importance of character abilities:
      - Example: a character that produces no noise even on a roll of 6
    - Began shifting the digital version away from instant loss:
      - Instead of losing when the noise maxes out, a monster entity begins approaching players
    - Brainstormed room interaction mechanics:
      - Event spaces placed around all sides of the room
      - Players must land on these spaces to reveal or interact with rooms

#### Learnings
  - Playtesting revealed that pacing is critical in maintaining engagement
  - Systems like the noise meter must be balanced between tension and fairness
  - Giving players control over risk (instead of constant penalties) improves gameplay experience
  - Physical prototypes help expose flaws that are not obvious in design
  - Transitioning from physical to digital requires rethinking mechanics, not just copying them
